About Us

Built for students who need clarity, speed, and accountability.

The Campus Credit Collective (operating as Stucha Loans) is a premier, student-centric micro-lending initiative designed to bridge financial gaps within the university community. Founded on the principles of accessibility, speed, and absolute transparency, we provide short-term, reliable financial relief tailored specifically to the unique needs and timelines of tertiary students. We don't just lend; we help students build accountability and manage their campus liquidity seamlessly.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply for a loan?

Any currently enrolled tertiary student with valid identification, proof of enrollment, and a verifiable source of allowance or income.

How long does the approval process take?

Once a complete application with all required documents is submitted, review and disbursement typically take less than 24 hours.

Can I pay off my loan earlier than the agreed date?

Yes. Early repayments are welcomed and can help boost your internal credit score for future, higher-limit applications.

What happens if I am late with my payment?

We highly encourage reaching out to your allocated agent before the due date. Late payments attract a standard penalty fee and affect your eligibility for future credit.

Legal & Operational Fine Print

Important Notice: By signing and submitting this application, the applicant acknowledges, understands, and agrees to the legally binding terms outlined below.

5.1 Loan Repayment & Interest

The applicant agrees to repay the principal amount plus the agreed-upon fixed flat interest rate on or before the specified due date.

All repayments must be routed through our verified disbursement channels or directly to the authorized agent assigned to your account.

5.2 Late Payment Penalties & Default

Failure to clear the outstanding balance by the strict due date will result in an immediate, mandatory late penalty fee applied to the account.

In the event of prolonged non-payment or avoidance of communication, the business reserves the right to escalate the matter to next-of-kin, relevant university administrative bodies, or independent legal debt recovery services.

5.3 Data Privacy & Consent

The applicant grants permission to the management of The Campus Credit Collective to verify academic status, enrollment details, and any financial references provided.

All personal information, contact records, and documentation submitted will be held securely and kept completely confidential, used solely for credit assessment and recovery.

5.4 Declaration of Accuracy

The applicant certifies that all information provided in this form is true, accurate, and up to date. Providing fraudulent information or falsified documents will result in immediate disqualification and permanent blacklisting.
